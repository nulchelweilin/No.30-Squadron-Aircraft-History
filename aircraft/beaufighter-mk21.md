# Bristol Beaufighter Mk.21

## Technical Specifications
- **Role:** Heavy fighter, night fighter, ground attack
- **Crew:** 2 (pilot, observer/navigator)
- **Engines:** 2 × Bristol Hercules XIV radial engines, 1,650 hp each
- **Maximum Speed:** 330 mph (530 km/h) at 15,000 ft (4,600 m)
- **Range:** 1,470 mi (2,370 km)
- **Service Ceiling:** 26,500 ft (8,080 m)

## Armament
- **Fixed forward-firing guns:** 4 × 20 mm Hispano Mk.II cannons in nose
- **Wing-mounted guns:** 6 × .303 in (7.7 mm) Browning machine guns
- **External stores:** Up to 2,000 lb (910 kg) of bombs, rockets, or one 18-inch (457 mm) torpedo

## Operational History with No. 30 Squadron RAAF
No. 30 Squadron operated the Beaufighter Mk.21 primarily in the South West Pacific Theatre from 1943 onward. The aircraft was used for long‑range strike missions against Japanese shipping, airfield attacks, and coastal patrols.

## Notable Missions
- **Operation OBOE:** Support during the Borneo campaign.
- **Anti‑shipping strikes** in the Dutch East Indies.
